ABOUT

Bootstrap's grid system uses a technique called margin collapse. When two block-level elements (like a div) are adjacent, the bottom margin of the first element may collapse into the margin of the second element, causing the space between them to appear smaller than expected. In your case, if there are elements above or below the col-12 that also have margins, their margins may be collapsing.

When you use col-12, it takes the full width of the container. If you are applying a bottom margin (mb-5) to this column, it may seem like it is not being applied correctly if there are no elements below it to push it down.
